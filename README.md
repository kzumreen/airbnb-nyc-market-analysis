# Airbnb Data Visualization & Market Analysis (NYC)

A comprehensive data analysis of the New York City Airbnb market, focusing on rental trends across boroughs, competitive pricing in Queens, and ROI (Return on Investment) calculations for property renovations.

## Project Overview
This project analyzes Airbnb listing data to identify the most lucrative locations and property types in NYC. A specific case study is conducted for a property in Astoria, comparing it against competitors in Long Island City and Woodside to determine optimal pricing and renovation recovery timelines.

## Key Insights
* **Borough Comparison:** Manhattan leads the market with the highest number of rentals (10,969) and the highest average price ($301.20).
* **Affordability:** The Bronx offers the most affordable rentals, while Queens and Staten Island maintain similar mid-tier pricing.
* **Geospatial Trends:** Higher rental prices are directly correlated with proximity to major landmarks like Central Park, Grand Central Terminal, and Wall Street.
* **Competitive Analysis:** Analyzed 3-bedroom, 2-bath (3B2B) homes in Queens. Long Island City represents a premium competitor ($420.50 avg), while Woodside offers consistent, budget-friendly competition ($360.27 avg).

## ROI Analysis: The "Julie" Case Study
The project includes a financial feasibility study for a renovation project:
* **Renovation Cost:** $15,000
* **Target Post-Renovation Daily Rate:** $308.40
* **Goal:** Calculate the number of rental days required to break even on renovation investments based on competitive market data in Astoria and surrounding neighborhoods.

## Data Visualizations
The analysis utilizes several visualization techniques to communicate market trends:
* **Borough Distribution:** Comparative bar charts for rental types.
* **Heatmaps/Map Views:** Visualizing price density near NYC transit hubs and financial districts.
* **Price Clustering:** Identifying market saturation and pricing tiers in specific Queens neighborhoods.
